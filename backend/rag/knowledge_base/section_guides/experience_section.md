# Experience Section Guide

## Purpose of the Experience Section

The Experience section is the heart of your resume. It demonstrates your professional track record through specific, measurable achievements — not a list of job duties. Recruiters and ATS systems spend the most time parsing this section.

---

## Formatting Each Entry

Use this consistent structure for every role:

```
Job Title                                          Start Date – End Date
Company Name — City, State (or Remote)
• Achievement bullet 1
• Achievement bullet 2
• Achievement bullet 3–6
```

### Rules
- **Reverse chronological order** — most recent role first.
- **3–6 bullets per role** — more for recent/relevant roles, fewer for older ones.
- **Each bullet = 1–2 lines max** — if a bullet wraps to 3 lines, split or tighten it.
- **Bold the job title** (some formats bold the company — pick one and be consistent).

---

## Writing Achievement-Oriented Bullets

### The STAR Method (Compressed for Resumes)
- **Situation/Task**: Brief context (often implied by the job title).
- **Action**: What YOU specifically did — lead with an action verb.
- **Result**: Measurable outcome (number, percentage, dollar amount, time saved).

**Formula**: `[Action Verb] + [Specific Task/Project] + [Quantified Result]`

**Example**: Redesigned the customer onboarding flow, reducing time-to-activation from 14 days to 3 days and increasing 30-day retention by 22%.

### The CAR Method (Context–Action–Result)
An alternative to STAR, useful when the context is not obvious from the title:

**Example**: Inherited a legacy monolith with 45-minute build times (Context). Decomposed it into 8 microservices with independent CI/CD pipelines (Action). Reduced build time to 4 minutes and deployment frequency from weekly to 15+ times/day (Result).

---

## Quantification in Experience Bullets

Every bullet should include at least one number when possible:

| Metric Type   | Example                                              |
|---------------|------------------------------------------------------|
| Revenue       | Generated $2.4M in new annual recurring revenue      |
| Cost savings  | Reduced vendor spend by $180K through contract renegotiation |
| Efficiency    | Decreased report generation time from 6 hours to 20 minutes |
| Scale         | Managed 150+ client accounts across 3 regions        |
| Quality       | Maintained 99.9% SLA uptime across production systems |
| Speed         | Delivered project 4 weeks ahead of 16-week timeline  |
| Growth        | Grew team from 3 to 12 engineers over 18 months      |

If you lack exact numbers, use reasonable estimates, ranges, or frequency indicators.

## Relevance Filtering

Not every task you performed belongs on your resume. For each bullet, ask:
1. **Does this support my candidacy for the target role?** If no, remove it.
2. **Does this differentiate me from other candidates?** Generic duties do not.
3. **Can I quantify it?** Quantified bullets are almost always worth keeping.

### What to Cut
- Routine duties obvious from the job title ("Answered phones," "Attended meetings")
- Responsibilities that do not relate to the target role
- Bullets that repeat the same skill without adding new information

### What to Keep
- Achievements that mirror the target job description's requirements
- Results that demonstrate scope, impact, and leadership
- Technical skills, tools, and methodologies mentioned in the job posting

## Handling Different Experience Scenarios

### Short Tenure (< 1 Year)
Include the role if it is relevant and you achieved something measurable. If you were laid off or the role was a contract, note "(Contract)" or "(Company acquired)" after the company name.

### Employment Gaps
- Do not try to hide gaps by omitting months — experienced recruiters will notice.
- If the gap was productive (freelancing, caregiving, coursework), create an entry:
  ```
  Freelance Marketing Consultant                     Mar 2023 – Sep 2023
  Self-Employed — Remote
  • Delivered social media strategy for 4 small business clients, growing average engagement by 45%.
  ```

### Promotions Within the Same Company
Show progression by listing each title separately under the company name:
```
Company Name — City, State                          Jan 2019 – Present
Senior Software Engineer                            Jul 2021 – Present
• [Senior-level achievements]

Software Engineer                                   Jan 2019 – Jun 2021
• [Earlier-level achievements]
```

### Very Long Career (15+ Years)
- Detail the last 10–15 years fully.
- Consolidate earlier roles into a brief "Earlier Experience" section:
  ```
  Earlier Experience: Held progressive roles in sales at Company A (2005–2010) and Company B (2002–2005).
  ```

## Common Experience Section Mistakes

1. **Starting bullets with "Responsible for"** — Always lead with an action verb.
2. **Using the same verb repeatedly** — Vary your verbs across bullets.
3. **Listing technologies without context** — "Used Python" is weak; "Built fraud detection pipeline in Python processing 2M daily transactions" is strong.
4. **Including every job since high school** — Only include relevant roles from the last 10–15 years.
5. **Copying job descriptions verbatim** — Recruiters can tell. Personalize each bullet with your specific contributions and results.
