# Resume Tips for Healthcare & Medical Roles

## General Guidelines
*   **Credentials First:** Your licenses and certifications (RN, MD, BLS, ACLS, etc.) are the most important part of your resume. Place them immediately after your name or in a highly visible section at the top.
*   **Patient Volume & Ratios:** Quantify your experience by stating patient loads (e.g., "Managed a 1:5 nurse-to-patient ratio on a busy Med-Surg unit").
*   **Facility Type:** Specify the type of facility you worked in (Level I Trauma Center, outpatient clinic, 500-bed acute care hospital). This provides crucial context.
*   **Keywords:** Include specific medical terminology, equipment used, and EHR systems (Epic, Cerner).

## Nursing & Patient Care
*   **Unit Specifics:** Highlight the specific units you have experience in (ICU, ER, NICU, L&D).
*   **Procedures:** List specific procedures you are proficient in (IV insertion, phlebotomy, wound care).
*   **Patient Outcomes:** If possible, mention improvements in patient satisfaction scores or reductions in infection rates on your unit.

## Physicians & Advanced Practice Providers
*   **Clinical Rotations:** For new grads, list your clinical rotations, highlighting those most relevant to the specialty you are applying for.
*   **Research & Publications:** Include a dedicated section for research, publications, and presentations if applying for academic or research-heavy roles.
*   **Leadership:** Mention roles like Chief Resident, committee memberships, or quality improvement initiatives.

## Healthcare Administration
*   **Regulatory Compliance:** Highlight experience with JCAHO, HIPAA, and CMS regulations.
*   **Financial Management:** Quantify budget sizes managed, cost-saving initiatives, and revenue cycle improvements.
*   **Quality Improvement:** Discuss specific QA/QI projects you led and their impact on patient outcomes or operational efficiency.

## Allied Health (PT, OT, Techs)
*   **Modality Expertise:** Specify the types of treatments, equipment, or modalities you are trained on.
*   **Caseload:** Mention the average number of patients seen per day.
*   **Interdisciplinary Collaboration:** Highlight your ability to work with physicians, nurses, and other therapists to develop care plans.

## Essential Action Verbs
Administered, Assessed, Charted, Diagnosed, Evaluated, Monitored, Rehabilitated, Triaged, Advocated, Educated, Implemented, Stabilized.
