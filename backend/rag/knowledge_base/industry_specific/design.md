# Resume Tips for Design Roles (UX/UI, Graphic Design, Product Design)

## General Guidelines
*   **Your Resume IS a Design Test:** The layout, typography, and visual hierarchy of your resume are just as important as the content. It must look impeccable.
*   **The Portfolio is King:** Your portfolio link must be prominent and clickable. Make sure the link is easy to read (e.g., yourname.com).
*   **Balance Aesthetics with ATS:** While a highly creative resume is great for handing to a hiring manager, ensure you also have an ATS-friendly, single-column version (like the Minimalist or Creative templates here) for online applications. Avoid overly complex graphics or skill progress bars that ATS cannot read.
*   **Show the Process, Not Just the Output:** Don't just list what you designed; describe *how* you designed it (research, wireframing, prototyping, testing).

## UX/UI & Product Design
*   **User-Centered Metrics:** Quantify the impact of your designs on the user experience (e.g., "Redesigned checkout flow, reducing cart abandonment by 18%," "Increased user engagement by 25% through simplified navigation").
*   **Tools:** List industry-standard tools (Figma, Sketch, Adobe XD, Principle, Framer, Miro).
*   **Collaboration:** Emphasize how you worked with cross-functional teams, particularly engineers and product managers, to bring designs to life.

## Graphic Design & Visual Design
*   **Scope of Work:** Detail the types of assets you created (e.g., "Designed branding identity, marketing collateral, and packaging for 15+ product launches").
*   **Brand Consistency:** Highlight your ability to maintain and evolve brand guidelines across multiple touchpoints.
*   **Client Management:** If you have agency or freelance experience, mention your ability to manage client expectations and present design concepts.

## Essential Action Verbs
Conceptualized, Designed, Prototyped, Wireframed, Illustrated, Redesigned, Streamlined, Visualized, Iterated, Conducted (Research), Crafted, Orchestrated.
