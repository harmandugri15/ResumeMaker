# Resume Tips for Marketing & Advertising Roles

## General Guidelines
*   **Show, Don't Just Tell:** As a marketer, your resume is a marketing document. It must be impeccably written, visually appealing, and persuasive.
*   **Metrics are Mandatory:** Marketing is highly data-driven. You must quantify your results using metrics like ROI, ROAS, CPA, conversion rates, traffic growth, and engagement rates.
*   **Campaign Scope:** Describe the scale of your campaigns (budget size, target audience size, geographic reach).
*   **Tools & Platforms:** Explicitly list the software you use (Google Analytics, HubSpot, Salesforce, Facebook Ads Manager, SEO tools like Ahrefs/SEMrush).

## Digital Marketing & SEO
*   **Traffic & Conversion:** Focus on how much traffic you drove and how much of it converted. (e.g., "Increased organic traffic by 45% YoY and improved conversion rate by 2%").
*   **Keyword Strategy:** Mention specific successes in ranking for competitive keywords.
*   **Channel Expertise:** Clarify your proficiency across different channels (Email, Social, Paid Search, Organic).

## Content Marketing & Copywriting
*   **Engagement Metrics:** Highlight metrics like time on page, social shares, open rates, and click-through rates (CTR).
*   **Content Volume:** Mention the quantity and types of content produced (e.g., "Managed an editorial calendar producing 4 blog posts and 1 whitepaper per week").
*   **Link to Portfolio:** Include a link to your published writing samples.

## Product Marketing
*   **Go-to-Market (GTM):** Detail your experience launching new products or features, including positioning, messaging, and sales enablement.
*   **Market Research:** Mention your experience conducting competitor analysis, user interviews, and win/loss analysis.
*   **Cross-Functional Leadership:** Highlight how you worked with Product, Sales, and Customer Success to drive adoption.

## Brand Management & PR
*   **Brand Awareness:** Use metrics like share of voice, media impressions, or brand sentiment.
*   **Crisis Management:** Briefly mention successful navigation of PR crises if applicable.
*   **Agency Management:** If you managed external creative or PR agencies, state the budget and scope.

## Essential Action Verbs
Amplified, Captivated, Converted, Generated, Launched, Positioned, Promoted, Segmented, Targeted, Optimized, Spearheaded, Cultivated.
