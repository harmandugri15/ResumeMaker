# Resume Tips for Engineering Roles (Mechanical, Civil, Electrical, etc.)

## General Guidelines
*   **Highlight Technical Proficiency:** Be specific about the software and tools you use (AutoCAD, SolidWorks, MATLAB, ANSYS, Revit).
*   **Project Lifecycles:** Describe your involvement in different phases of projects: conceptualization, design, prototyping, testing, manufacturing, and maintenance.
*   **Compliance and Standards:** Mention familiarity with industry standards (e.g., ISO, ASME, IEEE, OSHA codes).
*   **Quantify Impact:** Use numbers to show efficiency improvements, cost reductions, or performance gains (e.g., "Redesigned component X, reducing manufacturing cost by 15% and weight by 10%").

## Mechanical Engineering
*   **Design to Manufacturing:** Emphasize experience taking designs from CAD models to physical prototypes and mass production (DFM/DFA).
*   **Analysis and Simulation:** Detail your experience with FEA (Finite Element Analysis) or CFD (Computational Fluid Dynamics) to optimize designs.
*   **Materials Selection:** Mention specific materials you have worked with if relevant to the role (e.g., composites, high-temperature alloys).

## Civil & Structural Engineering
*   **Scale of Projects:** Always state the budget size, scope, and type of projects you've worked on (e.g., "$50M commercial high-rise," "municipal water treatment facility").
*   **Permitting and Regulations:** Highlight experience navigating local building codes, zoning laws, and environmental regulations.
*   **Field Experience:** Mention if you have on-site experience managing contractors, performing inspections, or resolving constructability issues.

## Electrical Engineering
*   **System Complexity:** Describe the complexity of the systems you design (e.g., voltage levels, number of components, power requirements).
*   **PCB Design vs. Power Systems:** Clarify your niche. If you do PCB design, list the layers and frequencies. If power, list the capacity and grid interaction.
*   **Testing and Validation:** Detail the equipment and methodologies used to validate electrical systems.

## Essential Action Verbs
Engineered, Drafted, Simulated, Fabricated, Calibrated, Inspected, Optimized, Modeled, Prototyped, Validated, Specified, Constructed.
