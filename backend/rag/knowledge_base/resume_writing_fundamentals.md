# Resume Writing Fundamentals

## Core Principles of Effective Resume Writing

A resume is a marketing document, not a biography. Every line must answer one question: "Why should this employer interview me?" The strongest resumes are concise, achievement-oriented, and tailored to the target role.

### The Three Laws of Resume Writing

1. **Relevance over completeness** — Include only what supports your candidacy for *this* role.
2. **Impact over responsibility** — Show what you achieved, not just what you were assigned.
3. **Clarity over creativity** — Recruiters spend 6–8 seconds on an initial scan. Make every second count.

---

## The STAR Method for Bullet Points

STAR (Situation, Task, Action, Result) is the gold standard for writing achievement-oriented bullets. In resume bullets, compress STAR into one to two lines:

- **Situation/Task**: Brief context (optional if obvious from the job title).
- **Action**: What *you* specifically did — use a strong action verb.
- **Result**: Quantified outcome wherever possible.

**Formula**: `[Action Verb] + [What You Did] + [Quantified Result/Impact]`

**Example (Weak):** Responsible for managing the sales team.
**Example (Strong):** Led a 12-person sales team to exceed quarterly revenue targets by 18%, generating $2.4M in new business.

---

## Quantification: The #1 Differentiator

Numbers transform vague claims into credible achievements. Quantify with:

- **Revenue/Cost**: "Reduced operational costs by $150K annually"
- **Percentages**: "Improved customer retention by 23%"
- **Volume/Scale**: "Processed 500+ support tickets weekly"
- **Time**: "Decreased deployment time from 4 hours to 20 minutes"
- **Rankings**: "Ranked #2 out of 45 sales representatives"

If you cannot find exact numbers, use defensible estimates: "approximately," "~," or ranges.

---

## Action Verbs: Start Every Bullet Right

Never begin a bullet with "Responsible for," "Duties included," or "Helped with." Instead, lead with a powerful, specific action verb.

| Weak Opening           | Strong Alternative                        |
|------------------------|-------------------------------------------|
| Responsible for sales  | Drove $1.2M in annual recurring revenue   |
| Helped with marketing  | Launched three multi-channel campaigns     |
| Was in charge of team  | Directed a cross-functional team of 8     |

Use past tense for previous roles and present tense for your current role.

## Formatting Rules

### Layout & Design
- Use **consistent fonts**: one professional sans-serif font (Calibri, Arial, Helvetica) at 10–12pt for body text, 13–16pt for headings.
- Maintain **0.5–1 inch margins** on all sides.
- Use **clear section headings** with visual separation (bold, slightly larger font, or a thin horizontal rule).
- Align all dates flush-right; keep company names and titles flush-left.
- Use **bullet points** (not paragraphs) for experience and achievements.

### Consistency Checks
- Date formats must be uniform: either "Jan 2022 – Mar 2024" or "01/2022 – 03/2024" — never mix.
- Punctuation at the end of bullets: either always use periods or never — pick one and be consistent.
- Job title formatting (bold, italic, etc.) should be identical across all entries.

## Resume Length Guidelines

| Experience Level       | Recommended Length |
|------------------------|--------------------|
| Student / Entry-Level  | 1 page (strict)    |
| 3–10 years experience  | 1 page preferred, 2 pages acceptable |
| 10+ years / Senior     | 2 pages maximum    |
| Academic CV            | No strict limit     |
| Federal Resume         | 3–5 pages (specific format) |

**Rule of thumb**: If removing a section or bullet does not weaken your candidacy for this specific role, remove it.

---

## Document Structure (Recommended Order)

1. **Header** — Name, phone, email, LinkedIn, portfolio/GitHub (if relevant)
2. **Professional Summary** — 2–4 sentence snapshot (skip "Objective" statements)
3. **Skills** — Categorized technical and domain skills
4. **Experience** — Reverse-chronological work history
5. **Education** — Degrees, certifications, relevant coursework
6. **Projects** (optional) — Especially valuable for students and career changers
7. **Additional** (optional) — Awards, publications, volunteer work, languages

Reorder sections strategically: if you are a recent graduate, Education may come before Experience.

---

## Tailoring for Every Application

Generic resumes underperform. For each application:

1. Study the job description and extract **keywords** (skills, tools, qualifications).
2. Mirror those keywords naturally in your summary, skills, and bullet points.
3. Reorder bullets within each role to surface the most relevant achievements first.
4. Adjust your Professional Summary to reflect the target role's language and priorities.
