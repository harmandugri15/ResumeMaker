# Common Resume Mistakes and How to Fix Them

## Content Mistakes

### 1. Using "Responsible for" Instead of Action Verbs
**Problem**: "Responsible for managing a team of 5 developers."
**Fix**: "Managed a team of 5 developers, delivering 3 product releases on schedule."
Start every bullet with a strong, specific action verb.

### 2. Listing Duties Instead of Achievements
**Problem**: Describing what the job required rather than what you accomplished.
**Fix**: Use the STAR formula — every bullet should show an action and its measurable result.

### 3. No Quantification
**Problem**: "Improved sales performance significantly."
**Fix**: "Increased regional sales by 34% ($1.8M) within 6 months." Always include numbers: percentages, dollar amounts, time saved, volume handled.

### 4. Using Passive Voice
**Problem**: "Reports were generated on a weekly basis."
**Fix**: "Generated weekly performance reports for 12 stakeholders, driving a 15% improvement in response time."

### 5. Including an Objective Statement Instead of a Summary
**Problem**: "Objective: Seeking a challenging role where I can grow."
**Fix**: Replace with a Professional Summary: "Results-driven marketing manager with 7+ years of experience in digital campaigns, driving $3M+ in attributed revenue."

### 6. Including Irrelevant Experience
**Problem**: Listing every job you have ever held, including unrelated summer jobs from 10+ years ago.
**Fix**: Include only roles relevant to the target position. Consolidate old, unrelated roles into a single "Earlier Experience" line if needed.

### 7. Using Personal Pronouns
**Problem**: "I managed a team" or "My responsibilities included."
**Fix**: Omit "I" and "my." Start directly with the verb: "Managed a team of 8 engineers."

### 8. Generic, Non-Tailored Content
**Problem**: Sending the same resume for every application.
**Fix**: Customize your summary, skills, and bullet order for each job description. Mirror keywords from the posting.

### 9. Buzzwords Without Evidence
**Problem**: "Dynamic, results-oriented team player with a proven track record."
**Fix**: Replace buzzwords with specific evidence: "Collaborated with 4 cross-functional teams to launch a product adopted by 10,000 users in 90 days."

### 10. Missing or Broken Contact Information
**Problem**: Outdated phone number, unprofessional email (partyguy99@email.com), or no LinkedIn.
**Fix**: Use a professional email (firstname.lastname@email.com). Include phone, email, LinkedIn URL, and portfolio if relevant.

## Formatting Mistakes

### 11. Inconsistent Date Formatting
**Problem**: "Jan 2022 – March 2024" mixed with "2020-2021."
**Fix**: Use one format everywhere. Recommended: "Mon YYYY – Mon YYYY" (e.g., "Jan 2022 – Mar 2024").

### 12. Walls of Text (No Bullet Points)
**Problem**: Long paragraphs describing job duties.
**Fix**: Use 3–6 concise bullet points per role. Each bullet should be 1–2 lines maximum.

### 13. Overly Creative or Complex Layouts
**Problem**: Multi-column designs, infographics, images, and colored sidebars.
**Fix**: Use a clean, single-column layout. ATS systems cannot parse tables, text boxes, or images reliably.

### 14. Inconsistent Formatting of Job Entries
**Problem**: One job has the company name bolded, another has the title bolded; date alignment varies.
**Fix**: Use a strict template. Every job entry should follow the identical format: Title, Company, Location, Date.

### 15. Using Headers/Footers for Contact Info
**Problem**: Name and email placed in the document header or footer.
**Fix**: Place all contact information in the main document body. Many ATS parsers skip headers and footers.

### 16. Too Long or Too Short
**Problem**: A new graduate with a 3-page resume, or a 15-year veteran with half a page.
**Fix**: Students/entry-level: 1 page. Mid-career (3–10 years): 1–2 pages. Senior (10+ years): 2 pages max.

## Strategic Mistakes

### 17. Not Including a Skills Section
**Problem**: Relying only on experience bullets to convey skills.
**Fix**: Add a dedicated Skills section near the top. List technical skills, tools, and platforms that the ATS can match against the job posting.

### 18. Listing Every Skill You Have Ever Learned
**Problem**: A skills section with 50+ items, many irrelevant to the target role.
**Fix**: Curate 10–20 skills most relevant to the specific job. Group them by category (Languages, Frameworks, Tools, etc.).

### 19. Gaps with No Explanation
**Problem**: Unexplained 12-month gap raises red flags.
**Fix**: Briefly note what you did: freelancing, caregiving, professional development, coursework. Address it naturally within your experience section or summary.

### 20. Including Salary Information or "References Available Upon Request"
**Problem**: Both are outdated and waste valuable space.
**Fix**: Remove entirely. References are provided separately when requested. Salary is discussed during interviews.

### 21. Typos and Grammatical Errors
**Problem**: Even one typo signals carelessness, especially for detail-oriented roles.
**Fix**: Proofread multiple times. Use grammar tools (Grammarly, spell check). Have another person review it.

### 22. Including Photos or Personal Details
**Problem**: Adding a headshot, age, marital status, or nationality (in US/UK/Canada/Australia).
**Fix**: Remove all personal identifiers. In these markets, photos invite unconscious bias and may trigger automatic rejection by some company policies.

### 23. Using File Names Like "resume_final_v3.docx"
**Problem**: Unprofessional file names are visible to recruiters.
**Fix**: Use "FirstName_LastName_Resume.pdf" or "FirstName_LastName_TargetRole_Resume.docx."

### 24. Outdated Technical Skills
**Problem**: Listing obsolete technologies (e.g., Adobe Flash, Visual Basic 6, Windows XP).
**Fix**: Remove skills that are no longer industry-relevant unless the specific job posting requests them. Focus on current, in-demand technologies.
